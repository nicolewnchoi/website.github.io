About

Hi there! My name is Wanpil Nicole Choi. I am an undergraduate pursuing a dual degree in computer science and art & design at the University of Michigan-Ann Arbor. I explore and build new ways to combine both disciplines of engineering and art to create pleasurable experiences for the user and promote humanity in the age of technology.

This is reflected in my projects where they center around the wellbeing of the user. For example, using mixed reality to create a detailed visualization of the pain points to help the users communicate their illness better and augmented reality to promote users’ wellbeing through nurturing virtual plants.

I’m currently focused on XR development, game development, digital animations, and learning artificial intelligence, computer vision, and UX/UI of web pages. As a game developer at AiR Play. I designed and developed a new AR-projector based game using C# in Unity for the launch of iGYM, AiR Play’s AR system to be launched to schools and recreational facilities. I also enjoy learning and exploring artificial intelligence. As a research scholar at Google’s Computer Science Research Program, I work with a Google mentor in achieving new research pathways in combination with virtual reality and artificial intelligence.

When I’m not working, I love solo traveling, exploring new restaurants, drawing digital art, skiing, and playing video games!

Daily Daisies

Plant Selection Screen

AR Scene with the selected plant

Daily Daisies is Alternate Reality Initiative’s year-long project where members developed and designed an augmented reality wellness app. The app aims to promote the user’s wellbeing through a generation of weekly and daily wellness tasks to nurture a collection of augmented reality plants. I guided members to design the user interface and gamification features to improve the wellness app’s user experience and encourage users to take care of their virtual plants often.

Painpal

A VR scene that demonstrates the user's burning pain

A treatment game "Star Stretch" that helps patients stretch their shoulders

The patient's view of the website designed in Figma visualizing the areas of pain and expected healing period.

PainPal is a mixed reality application to create a detailed visualization of the user’s pain points, allowing them to see exactly where and how your body is affected. With this information, PainPal suggests personalized exercises and stretches tailored to their specific needs, helping them to reduce pain and improve their overall well-being. I designed a website visualizing the patient’s areas of pain and the expected healing period accompanying the main PainPal application. Our team won the semifinalists for the best VR application in ensuring healthy lives and promoting wellbeing for all ages.
